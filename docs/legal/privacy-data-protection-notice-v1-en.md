## 1. Applicable data protection frameworks

O7 Digital Consulting aims to apply privacy and data protection principles aligned with the main international regulations applicable to digital services, including:

- **European Union / EEA:** General Data Protection Regulation, known as GDPR.
- **Canada:** Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act, known as PIPEDA.
- **United States:** applicable federal, state and sector-specific privacy and data security rules.
- **Mexico:** Federal Law on the Protection of Personal Data Held by Private Parties, known as LFPDPPP.

The GDPR regulates the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data in the European Union and defines obligations for controllers and processors.
PIPEDA sets fair information principles for the collection, use and disclosure of personal information in Canadian private-sector activities.
In the United States, the FTC recommends that businesses know what personal information they collect, keep only what they need, protect it, dispose of it properly and prepare for security incidents.
In Mexico, the LFPDPPP governs the processing of personal data held by private parties and requires clear privacy information for data subjects.

## 6. AI processing notice

Olivia IA uses artificial intelligence to generate responses and assist users. Depending on the configuration, messages submitted through the chat may be processed by an AI service provider in order to generate a response.

Users should not submit sensitive personal information, confidential legal information, medical data, financial credentials, passwords or any information that is not necessary for the commercial request.

O7 Digital Consulting recommends that clients clearly inform visitors that they are interacting with an AI assistant.

## 7. Data security

O7 Digital Consulting applies reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, disclosure, alteration or destruction.

These measures may include access control, secure hosting, encrypted communications where available, limited access to administrative tools and separation of client environments where applicable.

No digital system can be guaranteed to be completely secure. Clients remain responsible for managing access to their own accounts and ensuring that their users follow appropriate security practices.

## 8. Data retention

Personal data should be retained only for as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, unless a longer retention period is required by law, accounting obligations, contractual obligations or legitimate business needs.

The client is responsible for defining the applicable retention rules for its own prospects, customers and CRM data.
